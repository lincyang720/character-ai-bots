Getting good at AI roleplay is a skill. The difference between a flat, boring conversation and an incredibly immersive experience comes down to how you write your messages. Here are 15 tips that will instantly improve your AI roleplay.

Writing Tips

1. Always Include Actions

Wrap physical descriptions and actions in *asterisks*. This gives the AI visual context and leads to richer responses.

2. Write at Least 2-3 Sentences

Short responses = short AI responses. Give the bot material to work with. Include dialogue, actions, and internal thoughts.

3. Use Sensory Details

Mention what you see, hear, smell, or feel. "The air smells like smoke and old books" creates atmosphere instantly.

4. Don't Control the AI's Character

Write what YOUR character does. Let the AI write its own character's reactions. This creates genuine surprises.

5. Use (( OOC )) for Out-of-Character Talk

Need to redirect the story? Use double parentheses: ((Can we change the setting to a castle?))

Story Tips

6. Start with a Strong Scene

Your first message sets the tone for everything. Make it count. Describe the setting, the mood, and how the encounter begins.

7. Introduce Conflict Early

Conflict drives stories. A mystery to solve, a secret to uncover, a danger to face — give the conversation stakes.

8. "Yes, And" Everything

Accept what the AI introduces and build on it. If the bot mentions a strange noise, investigate it. This keeps the story flowing.

9. Let the AI Surprise You

Don't plan every detail. The best roleplay moments come from unexpected AI responses that take the story in new directions.

10. Use Time Skips

Not every moment needs to be played out. "Three hours later, as the sun sets..." keeps the pacing tight.

Character Tips

11. Give Your Character a Personality

Are you brave? Sarcastic? Nervous? Picking 2-3 traits makes your character consistent and the AI's responses more tailored.

12. Have a Motivation

What does your character want? Revenge? Love? Knowledge? A clear goal gives the conversation direction.

13. Show, Don't Tell

Instead of "I'm angry," write *slams fist on the table, jaw clenched* "Say that again." Let actions convey emotions.

14. Play Off the AI's Personality

If you're chatting with a tsundere bot, tease them. With a yandere, mention other people. Play into the dynamic.

15. Try Different Genres

Don't stick to one type. Try fantasy quests, slow-burn romance, sci-fi adventures, and action scenarios. Variety makes you a better roleplayer.
